The Process

How We Get Your Child Enrolled

A clear, step-by-step breakdown of the official credential recognition process in the Dominican Republic — and exactly what we do for you at every stage.

1
📤

You Send Your Documents by Email

Send us your child's certified school transcripts and diplomas by email — already notarized, legalized, and apostilled (Hague Convention) in your country of origin.

Done by you
2
🌐

We Deliver Your Documents to an Authorized Translator

We deliver your documents to a certified public translator (intérprete público autorizado) recognized by Dominican law to translate them into Spanish.

Done by us
3
🏛️

We Certify the Translation

We personally present the translated documents to the Procuraduría General de la República to have the translator's signature officially certified — a legally required step before submitting to the Ministry.

Done by us
4
📁

We Submit Everything to the Ministry

We compile your complete file — original apostilled records, certified translations, birth certificate, and all required supporting documents — and submit them in person to the Ministry of Education's Dirección de Acreditación in Santo Domingo.

Done by us
5
📄

We Pick Up the Provisional Authorization

Approximately 10 business days from submitting documentation, we return to the Ministry to retrieve the Autorización de Inscripción Provisional — the document that officially allows your child to enroll in school right away.

~10 business days from submitting documentation
6
🎓

Your Child Enrolls in School

We deliver the Provisional Authorization to you. With this document, your child can officially enroll in any institution registered with the Ministry of Education in the Dominican Republic — private or public.

Your child can start!
7
✅

We Pick Up the Definitive Authorization

Months later, the Ministry issues the Autorización Definitiva — the permanent, official recognition of your child's foreign credentials. We go back to retrieve it and deliver it to you. No extra charge.

Included in the $300
What You Need

Documents Required by the Ministry of Education

These are the official requirements from the Dirección de Acreditación y Titulación de Estudios of the Dominican Republic's Ministry of Education.

Important: All original documents must be legalized and apostilled (Hague Convention) in your country of origin before we can begin the process. We'll explain exactly what this means during your consultation.
📜
Original School Documents — Apostilled
Original documents from your child's school: signed by the educational institution, the Ministry of Education of your country, and bearing the Hague Apostille stamp.
📚
Primary Education Certification (6th Grade)
Official certification that your child completed primary school (6th grade level or equivalent), or official grade transcripts demonstrating completion.
📊
Year-by-Year Secondary Transcripts
Complete academic records for each year of secondary school completed (e.g., if 4 years: grades for years 1, 2, 3, and 4). Include any diplomas, certificates, or titles if available.
🪦
Original Birth Certificate
Your child's original birth certificate — also apostilled if it is a foreign document.
🌐
Certified Spanish Translation (if applicable)
If your documents are not in Spanish, they must be translated by an authorized public interpreter. The translation must be legalized by the Procuraduría General de la República — a step we manage entirely for you.
